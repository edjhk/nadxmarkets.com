Regulatory Capture: Why Markets Never Get Fixed

Mancur Olson explained it in 1965. The SEC has confirmed it every decade since. Why concentrated interests defeat diffuse majorities — every time — and what that means for the buy-side.

This essay uses the logic of collective action to explain why concentrated trading interests consistently outperform diffuse investor interests in regulatory contests.

The core claim is not that regulators are uniquely corrupt or uniquely incompetent. It is that the incentives are structurally misaligned — and have been since the first exchange demutualized.

The 2022 equity market structure reforms were the most ambitious overhaul in a generation. All four proposals were withdrawn in June 2025. That outcome was not a surprise. It was the predicted result of a system working exactly as designed.
