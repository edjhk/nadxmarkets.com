Publications and formal commentary.

This section gathers essays, formal submissions, and long-form commentary on market structure, settlement, exchange incentives, and the architecture of securities markets.

Why No Moon for Securities on the Blockchain

A case against the fantasy that tokenized securities will recreate ICO-style upside, and an argument that institutional economics and market structure matter far more than hype.

Regulatory Capture — Why Markets Never Get Fixed

An argument that market reform repeatedly fails because concentrated trading interests can organize, spend, and outlast the diffuse investor majority.

The Compact That Was Never Honored

A historical argument that demutualization severed the old bargain between trading privilege and market-making obligation, leaving the buy-side as customer and then product.

The Netting Myth

A challenge to the claim that T-instant settlement is blocked by economic reality, arguing that many of the loudest objections are defenses of the current intermediary structure.
